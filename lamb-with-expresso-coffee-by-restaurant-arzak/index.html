INGREDIENTES: (for 4 pers.)

     1.- To soak the raisins: 40 gr. Raisins, 15 gr.
Brandy, 35 gr. fried almonds, 1 small tomato, 20 gr. Cooked garlic, 15 gr.
Virgin olive oil, 1 soup spoon Spanish sherry vinagre (vinagre de Jerez), Salt
and pepper.

     2.- For the lamb: 800 gr. rack of lamb (200 gr. /
Unit.)

     3.- For the lamb and milk sauce: 500 gr. lamb bones, 2
onions, 1 clove of garlic, 1 shallot, ¼ l. water, ¼ l. lamb stock, 1 small
spring onion, ¼ dl. Milk, Salt, pepper and knapweed
shreds.

     4.- For the mint leaves: 12 mint leaves and ½ olive
oil.

     5.- For the veil of coffee with milk: 2 gr.
decaffeinated coffee powder, 8 gr. agar-agar, 1.5 icing sugar and 1.5 gr. milk
powder.  Plus: Virgin olive oil and Knapweed shreds

PREPARATION:

     1.- For the raisin concentrate: Crush all the
ingredients and season. Leave to one side.

     2.- For the lamb:
Clean the rack of lamb well and cut into portions. Spread the raisin concentrate
over the pieces and fry to a golden brown on both sides. Leave to one
side.

     3.- For the lamb and milk sauce: Colour the bones
slightly with a touch of oil. Add the garlic and onions cut julienne style and
fry lightly for a few minutes. Remove the grease and add the lamb stock and
water. Cook on a low heat for 2 hours. Drain. Add the milk and onion cut into
very fine rings together with the knapweed shreds.
Season.

     4.- For the mint leaves: Whiten the mint in boiling
water. Cool well. Spread the leaves and press flat. Cover with oil and leave to
one side.

     5.- For the veils of coffee with milk: Mix all the
ingredients and mix to a fine powder. Grease a plastic mould of about 10 x 20 cm
with a little oil. Sprinkle with the powder and shake out any excess. Cook the
mould face down in a steamer at 119 ºC for 0.2 minutes. Take the mould out of
the steamer and remove the veil from the mould.

FINAL AND PRESENTATION:

On the bottom of the plate place the mint leaves, well spread
out, together with the knapweed shreds and some drops of oil. In the centre of
the plate place the lamb coated with the veil giving the plate height and
volume.
Serve together with the sauce separately in a sauce boat. In front
of the diner pour the very hot sauce over the veil making it disappear
